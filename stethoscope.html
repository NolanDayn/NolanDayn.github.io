Digital Stethoscope

For our final year Capstone Engineering Project my teamate Connor and I chose to design a Digital Stethoscope. With the idea that it would allow medical or other proffesionals to amplify, dampen, record, store and visualize sounds. As an extra add on we are also working on training machine learning modules to detect heart murmurs and other sounds. At the time of writing this we are about a month away from completing the project.

Above is the PCB we designed using Altium and then later produced. It includes an ESP32 WROVER running a modified version of FreeRTOS which forms the communication and computation backbone of the design. Along with a MEMS I2S Microphone, Headphone Amp and DAC, battery charger, power supply and battery voltage monitoring circuit.

Here are pictures of the PCBs before they are populated and the horn we 3D printed and designed for the stethoscope. The horn was designed using equations and information provided by this very informative paper.
https://www.grc.com/acoustics/an-introduction-to-horn-theory.pdf
